# E-Prescribing Guide

Decoda integrates with DoseSpot to give providers the ability to electronically prescribe medications directly from the platform. When a provider opens e-prescribing, they're seamlessly signed into DoseSpot -- no separate login required.

<Info>
All prescribing workflows -- searching for medications, selecting pharmacies, managing refills, and Electronic Prescribing for Controlled Substances (EPCS) -- happen inside DoseSpot. This guide covers the Decoda side: getting access, setting permissions, and using the integration day-to-day.
</Info>

## Getting Access

To get started with e-prescribing:

1. Contact Decoda support at support@decodahealth.com
2. Request e-prescribing module enablement
3. Decoda will enable the module, set up your locations, and add your providers

Once everything is configured, providers with e-prescribing access can launch DoseSpot from several places in Decoda (covered below) and will see the e-prescribing banner at the top of the notifications panel.

## Where to launch e-prescribing

There are several places in Decoda that open DoseSpot. Which one you use depends on what you're trying to do.

### From the notifications panel

Open the notifications panel from the top bar. At the top of the panel you'll see an e-prescribing banner showing the count of pending items (transmission errors, refill requests, change requests). Click the banner to open DoseSpot.

When there are no pending items, the banner reads **Open e-prescribing** and opens the DoseSpot dashboard without a patient loaded -- useful for working through your queue or checking a prior prescription.

<Info>
The banner only appears for providers whose DoseSpot account has been confirmed. If a provider can't see it, see [Notification Banner Not Showing](#notification-banner-not-showing) below.
</Info>

### From a patient chart (most common)

Open the patient and stay on the **Overview** tab. Scroll to the **Medications** section. From here:

1. Click **Add Dose** at the top right of the medication table.
2. A small menu appears with two options: **Add Dose** (record a dose manually in Decoda) and **E-prescribe** (open DoseSpot for this patient).
3. Click **E-prescribe**.

DoseSpot opens in a new tab with the patient's chart already loaded.

<Info>
**Add Dose** is the visible button -- there is no standalone "E-prescribe" button on the chart. The E-prescribe option lives inside the Add Dose menu.
</Info>

The same **Add Dose → E-prescribe** menu appears anywhere doses can be added, including:

- **Care plan checklist** items in the right-hand sidebar
- **Medication questions** inside scribe notes
- **Weight-loss program** dose tables

### Reopening an existing prescription

If a medication was already sent through DoseSpot, you can jump back into it without going through Add Dose:

1. In the **Medications** section, find the row for the prescription. It will be marked with a **DoseSpot** badge.
2. Click the three-dot menu on the right.
3. Choose **Open in E-prescribing**.

### Syncing patient demographics manually

Above the medications table on the Overview tab, there's a **Sync to E-Prescribe** button.

This button **does not open DoseSpot.** It pushes the patient's current name, date of birth, address, phone, and height/weight to DoseSpot so the records stay in sync. Decoda also runs this sync automatically the first time you E-prescribe for a patient and on subsequent launches, so most clinics never need to press it. Use it if a patient's demographics changed and you want to update DoseSpot before opening it.

### Picking a location

If your tenant has more than one location with DoseSpot configured, Decoda will show a **Select Location for E-prescribing** dialog the first time you launch from a given action. Decoda pre-selects the best guess (your current location, or the patient's primary location). Confirm the choice and click **E-prescribe**.

If only one location is configured, the dialog is skipped.

## Patient Requirements

For the patient sync to succeed, the patient record in Decoda must have:

- First name and last name
- Date of birth
- Phone number
- Full address (street, city, state, zip code)

If any of these are missing, you'll see an error when trying to open e-prescribing. Update the patient's profile and try again.

## Prescription Tracking

When prescriptions are sent through DoseSpot, Decoda automatically creates dose records on the patient's chart. These records update as the pharmacy processes the prescription:

- **Sent** -- Prescription transmitted to the pharmacy
- **Filled** or **Partially Filled** -- Pharmacy has dispensed the medication
- **Completed** -- Prescription fully dispensed
- **Cancelled** or **Discontinued** -- Prescription no longer active

Doses linked to DoseSpot are marked with a **DoseSpot** badge in the patient's medication history, so you can distinguish e-prescribed medications from manually recorded ones.

## Troubleshooting

### "Clinician not confirmed" Error

This means the provider's account hasn't been fully confirmed yet. Contact Decoda support at support@decodahealth.com to get your clinician confirmed. This is a one-time step.

### Patient Sync Fails

The patient record is missing required information. Check that the patient has:

- First and last name
- Date of birth
- Phone number
- Complete address (street, city, state, zip code)

Update the missing fields and try again.

### E-prescribe Button Not Showing

Check the following:

1. **Module is enabled** -- The e-prescribing module must be active for your tenant. Contact Decoda support if you're unsure.
2. **Provider is set up** -- The provider must be configured for e-prescribing by Decoda. Contact support if a provider who should have access doesn't.

### Notification Banner Not Showing

The e-prescribing banner in the notifications panel only appears when the signed-in provider has a DoseSpot user ID linked to their Decoda account. If you don't see it:

1. Confirm e-prescribing is enabled for your tenant.
2. Confirm the provider has been set up by Decoda support and has completed their DoseSpot clinician confirmation and Duo two-factor enrollment.
3. If the provider can launch DoseSpot from a patient chart but the banner is still missing, contact Decoda support -- their account may need to be linked.

### Duo Two-Factor Authentication Enrollment Failing

If a provider is unable to enroll in Duo two-factor authentication within DoseSpot, they may have a pre-existing DoseSpot account from a previous clinic or platform. This blocks the enrollment process. Contact Decoda support at support@decodahealth.com so we can merge the accounts and resolve the issue.

### Notification Counts Not Updating

Notification counts are pushed from DoseSpot automatically. If counts seem stale:

1. Refresh the page
2. Check DoseSpot directly to verify the current count
3. Contact Decoda support if the issue persists

## What Happens Inside DoseSpot

Once DoseSpot opens, the following all happen within DoseSpot's interface:

- Searching for and selecting medications
- Choosing pharmacies
- EPCS (Electronic Prescribing for Controlled Substances) enrollment
- Managing refills and renewals
- Drug interaction warnings

If you run into any issues inside DoseSpot, contact Decoda support and we'll help resolve it.

## Support

For any e-prescribing questions or issues, contact Decoda support at support@decodahealth.com.

When reaching out, include:
- Your tenant name
- The provider involved
- Any error messages or screenshots
- What you were trying to do
